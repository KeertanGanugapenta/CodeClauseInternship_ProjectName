    About Weather

    Temperature: Temperature is a measure of how hot or cold the air is. It is typically measured in degrees Celsius (°C) or Fahrenheit (°F). Daily temperature fluctuations are caused by the amount of solar radiation received, time of day, and geographical location.

    Humidity: Humidity refers to the amount of water vapor present in the air. It is usually expressed as a percentage. High humidity can make the air feel muggy, while low humidity can make it feel dry. Humidity influences our comfort and can affect how hot or cold the air feels. It also plays a role in the formation of clouds and precipitation.

    Wind: Wind is the movement of air from areas of high pressure to low pressure. Wind speed is measured in units like miles per hour (mph) or kilometers per hour (km/h). Wind direction is given in compass points (e.g., north, south, east, west). Wind influences the distribution of temperature and moisture, and it can bring different weather patterns.
